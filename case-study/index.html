Strategic Challenge

Turk Telekom, one of Turkey's largest telecommunications companies, faced a critical security and operational challenge in managing secure access for 4,000+ global contractors across multiple jurisdictions. The company required a solution that could provide secure access to both web and desktop applications while maintaining strict data sovereignty and regulatory compliance requirements across different geographic regions.

As a major telecommunications provider serving millions of customers, Turk Telekom needed to ensure that their extensive contractor workforce could securely access critical business systems including ERP platforms, engineering tools, customer databases, and network management systems. The challenge was compounded by the need to maintain compliance with Turkish data protection laws while enabling seamless global operations.

The existing infrastructure was creating significant bottlenecks in contractor onboarding, limiting business agility, and generating substantial operational overhead for the IT security team. A comprehensive solution was needed that could scale globally while maintaining the highest security standards.

The Problem with Traditional Approaches

Turk Telekom's existing infrastructure created significant operational and financial burdens that were hindering business growth and creating security vulnerabilities. The company's analysis revealed three critical pain points that needed immediate attention:

Managed Hardware Approach:

The traditional approach of providing managed hardware to contractors was proving increasingly unsustainable as the company scaled globally.

  • • Procurement cycles averaging 3-4 weeks, delaying critical project starts
  • • Cross-border logistics complications affecting 15+ countries
  • • Hardware failure rates of 12% annually requiring immediate replacement
  • • Asset management overhead consuming 40% of IT support time

Legacy VDI Infrastructure:

The existing Virtual Desktop Infrastructure was creating bottlenecks and escalating costs as contractor numbers grew.

  • • VDI licensing costs increasing 25% annually with contractor growth
  • • Performance issues during peak usage hours affecting productivity
  • • Complex multi-tier architecture requiring specialized expertise
  • • Limited scalability for sudden contractor surge requirements

Business Impact

These infrastructure challenges were directly impacting Turk Telekom's ability to respond to market opportunities. New contractor onboarding delays were affecting project timelines, while the high operational overhead was diverting resources from strategic initiatives. The company needed a solution that could eliminate these bottlenecks while enhancing security posture.

Strategic Solution: Netzilo's Digital Enterprise Workplace

After evaluating multiple solutions, Turk Telekom selected Netzilo's comprehensive Digital Enterprise Workplace platform. This decision was driven by Netzilo's unique ability to provide unified protection for both web and desktop applications while eliminating the need for traditional VDI infrastructure and managed hardware.
